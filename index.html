About This Module

Topics in Biostastics is designed to expose students to some aspects of the application of statistics to biology, ecology and medicine. Each of these disciplines have a great need for statistical principles and methods as biological material is very variable. The course deals with 3 specific areas in which statistics are used.
• Pharmaceutical statistics: 12 lectures
(Dr. C. Gormley, School of Mathematical Sciences)
• Ecological statistics: 4 lectures
(Prof. J. Connolly, School of Mathematical Sciences)
• Medical statistics: 8 lectures
(Prof. L. Daly, School of Public Health & Population Science)

Lectures take place on Wednesday from 4pm to 6pm in room 550B. The course is examined by written exam at the end of Semester 2.
